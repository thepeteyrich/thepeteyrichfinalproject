Serving as the company's mascot and the eponymous protagonist of the series, Mario has appeared in over 200 video games since his creation. Actually, Miyamoto named Mario "Mr. Video" at first but thought giving him a real name would enable players to connect with him more. Mario was first created as a carpenter but it wasn't until Mario Bros. that Miyamoto decided that Mario should be a plumber with his brother since most of the levels were underground. Created 10.9.14

The Phillie Phanatic is the official mascot of the Philadelphia Phillies. He is a large, furry, green bi-pedal creature with an extendable tongue. If you ask me what kind of animal he is, I will say Phanatic. And I will be correct. End of discussion. According to his official biography, the Phanatic is originally from the Gal√°pagos Islands. Which is similar to North Philly. The Phanatic is acknowledged as one of the best ballpark mascots and is the only thing the @Phillies have going for them this season/decade. During the winter after the 1977 season, Dennis Lehman, who thought that the team needed a mascot similar to San Diego's Famous Chicken, created the Phanatic with help from Harrison/Erickson of NYC (now known as Acme Mascots), which had ties with Jim Henson's Muppets. The Phanatic debuted on April 25, 1978, at The Vet, when the Phils played the Chicago Cubs. The Phanatic rides around on an ATV, wanders the stadium, greeting fans and humorously mocking supporters of the opposition or the opposition themselves. His mother, Phoebe, occasionally appears on-field with the Phanatic. He also has a younger cousin Phred, who is rarely seen. Classic Phred. Black sheep of the family. Get a job Phred. In his book, current owner and former team VP Bill Giles wrote of the worst decision of his life when it came to the creation of the Phanatic. The design would cost $5,200 for both the costume and the copyright ownership, or $3,900 just for the costume with Harrison/Erickson retaining the copyright. Giles chose to just buy the costume. Five years later, when Giles and his group of investors bought the team from Ruly Carpenter, the franchise paid $250,000 to Harrison/Erickson for the copyright. Whoops-a-daisy. The Phanatic is one of only 2 mascots in the Halls of Cooperstown (the other being SD Chicken). Lastly, The Phanatic also has the dubious distinction of being the most sued mascot in sports. A true Philadelphian.
